## What's here

- `site/` — the Angular landing page (the thing that gets deployed)
- `firebase.json` / `.firebaserc` — Firebase Hosting config
- this README — plus a set of **productive site ideas** below

## Develop & deploy

```bash
cd site
npm install
npm start                 # local dev server at http://localhost:4200
npm run build             # production build -> site/dist/site/browser

# from repo root, after a build:
firebase deploy --only hosting
```

## Productive site ideas worth building

A menu of constructive directions — for your career, and for turning a hard
chapter into something that helps you and others. Each is something I can build
and deploy.

### Read this first — guardrails for anything public

Because you're on active probation tied to a protective order, **keep all of the
following out of anything public**, no exceptions:

- Names, photos, handles, or any reference to the other party or her family.
- The Exhibit A / B images (those live only in your private packet for counsel).
- Any claim that you're innocent or that the plea was coerced/"perjured."
- Anything that narrates the case in a way that could be read as directed at, or
  about, the protected person.

**Have Chris review anything that so much as touches the case before it ships.**
The ideas in Group B & C below are written to stay on the safe side of that line,
but counsel sign-off is the gate.

### Group A — Move your career forward (lowest risk, highest payoff)

1. **Professional portfolio** *(this repo)* — who you are, what you build, how to
   reach you. The single most useful site for getting hired. No case content.
2. **trenchwork.org product / business site** — if trenchwork is a product or
   service, a real marketing site: what it does, for whom, pricing, contact.
   Channels your energy into the business.
3. **Open-source / project showcase** — auto-pulls your public GitHub repos,
   shows what you ship. Demonstrates skill better than any résumé line.

### Group B — Help others with what you learned (constructive, general, no personal case details)

4. **Fair-chance employment resource** — a clean guide for job seekers with
   records: how to talk about it, know-your-rights under fair-chance/ban-the-box
   laws, employer lists. Helping others reads well and is genuinely useful.
5. **Record sealing & expungement guide** — a state-by-state explainer of
   eligibility and process, with links to official forms. Purely educational;
   no personal details. (Pursue your own sealing with Chris in parallel.)
6. **"Online conduct & protective orders" explainer** — a neutral, educational
   resource on how social-media activity intersects with protective orders and
   probation. General and sourced — not your story. Have counsel review.

### Group C — Personal growth (counsel-vetted only)

7. **Accountability / rebuilding blog** — short essays on owning a mistake and
   moving forward. Forward-looking, no third parties, no relitigating the case.
   Only with Chris's written sign-off on the framing.

### Not recommended

- A site narrating the case, asserting innocence, or publishing the exhibits or
  anyone's identity. It risks a fresh probation violation, undercuts your
  willful-contact argument, and is permanently indexed. Don't.
